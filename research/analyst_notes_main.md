## 🏛️ NATIONAL POWER STRUCTURE (2024–present)

### The Prabowo Coalition (KIM Plus)
The current governing coalition — Koalisi Indonesia Maju (KIM) Plus — controls an overwhelming parliamentary majority:
- **KIM parties:** Gerindra, Golkar, Demokrat, PAN, PKB, NasDem, PKS, PPP, Hanura, Perindo, Garuda, PSI
- **Opposition:** PDI-P (isolated after Jokowi split) + PKS (formally in KIM but sometimes critical)
- **PDI-P situation:** Megawati's party backed losing candidate Ganjar-Mahfud. Now in political wilderness. Jokowi was expelled from PDIP in 2024 — major rupture after 10-year alliance.

### Real Power Centers
1. **Prabowo Subianto** — President, Gerindra founder, former Kopassus commander. Controls TNI through influence.
2. **Joko Widodo ("Jokowi")** — Formally retired but real influence through: son Gibran (VP), allies in cabinet, Projo movement. PDIP-Jokowi split means he's now aligned with Prabowo camp.
3. **Megawati Soekarnoputri** — Ketum PDIP, elder stateswoman. Soekarno's daughter. Controls 110 DPR seats — largest party but in opposition. Strained relationship with almost everyone currently in power.
4. **Airlangga Hartarto** — Golkar. Family wealth from Astra International era. Controls economic ministry networks.
5. **Muhaimin Iskandar (Cak Imin)** — PKB. Controls NU-affiliated votes in Jatim, Jateng. Fragile relationship with actual NU leadership (Yahya Cholil Staquf's PBNU is NOT automatically PKB-aligned).
6. **Hashim Djojohadikusumo** — Prabowo's younger brother. Business empire (Arsari Group). NOT in government but critical influence. Mining, plantation interests.
7. **Hary Tanoesoedibjo** — Media king (MNC Group: RCTI, Global TV, Koran Sindo). Ketum Perindo. Political alignment: formerly SBY ally, now Prabowo coalition. Key media influence.
8. **Surya Paloh** — NasDem. Metro TV founder. Backed Anies then quietly joined Prabowo cabinet discussions. Media + political influence.

## ⚠️ KEY CONTROVERSIES DATABASE

### PRABOWO SUBIANTO
**Severity: 🔴 HIGH — Must display on profile with sources**

1. **1998 Activist Kidnappings (Penculikan Aktivis)**
   - Prabowo commanded Kopassus Tim Mawar which abducted 23 pro-democracy activists during 1997-1998
   - 9 activists never returned (presumed dead/disappeared)
   - 14 were released after being held and reportedly tortured
   - DKP (Honor Council) found Prabowo guilty of ordering kidnappings → dismissed from military Aug 1998
   - He was Pangkostrad (Army Strategic Reserve Commander) at the time, brother-in-law of Soeharto
   - **Status as of 2024:** Never criminally tried. Controversially promoted by Jokowi to Honorary General rank in 2023 (after serving as Menhan).
   - Sources: Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, Kontras NGO

2. **East Timor Operations (1980s-1990s)**
   - Served in East Timor multiple tours during occupation period
   - Accused of human rights violations against civilian population
   - Specific operations disputed but documented by international human rights groups

3. **Role in May 1998 Riots**
   - Disputed allegations about coordination with pro-riot elements during May 13-15 1998 Jakarta riots
   - 1,188+ people died, thousands of Chinese-Indonesian women raped
   - Prabowo denies involvement; investigation inconclusive
   - Context: The riots coincided with his removal and political downfall

4. **Paradise Papers (2017)**
   - Offshore business interests revealed in Panama/Paradise Papers leak
   - Specifically: land holdings via offshore entities

5. **Danantara Sovereign Wealth Fund (2025)**
   - Created new SWF managing state enterprise assets (~$900B claimed)
   - Critics: lacks independent oversight, concentrated under presidential control
   - Potential democratic backsliding concern

6. **Armed Forces Reform Act (TNI Law revision 2025)**
   - Expanded TNI's role in civilian government positions
   - Reversed reforms from Reformasi era
   - Critics: return to dwifungsi (dual military-civilian function) of Soeharto era
   - Passed March 2025 — major governance concern

### BUDI GUNAWAN (Kepala BIN)
**Severity: 🔴 HIGH**
- Nominated as Kapolri by Jokowi in 2015 → immediately became KPK suspect for gratifikasi (illegal gifts)
- KPK charged him with receiving Rp 57.2 billion in suspicious transactions 2004-2006 as head of Polri's personnel bureau
- Constitutional Court rejected KPK's jurisdiction → case dropped on technicality
- KPK considered this its biggest defeat ever; relationship with Polri deteriorated
- Still became Wakapolri, then Kepala BIN under Jokowi → retained by Prabowo
- **Assessment:** Represents systemic impunity in security apparatus

### AHMAD MUHDLOR ALI "GUS MUHDLOR" (Bupati Sidoarjo)
**Severity: 🔴 ACTIVE CASE**

- Case: Pemotongan (deduction) of incentive funds for civil servants (ASN) in Sidoarjo

- Initially fled KPK summons (hiding at father's pesantren) before surrendering
- **Status:** In court proceedings as of early 2025
- Parent: Son of ulama, PKB network in Sidoarjo
- Connection: Part of PKB machine but embarrassment for Cak Imin

### HASAN AMINUDDIN (eks-Wakil Gubernur Jatim)
**Severity: 🔴 TERPIDANA**
- Mantan Bupati Probolinggo (multiple terms), then Wakil Gubernur Jatim candidate

- **Significance:** Illustrates political dynasty + corruption intersection in Jatim

### SANUSI (Bupati Malang, terpidana)

## 🗺️ JAWA TIMUR POWER DYNAMICS

### Why Jatim Matters
- 40+ million voters — largest province after Jawa Barat and Jawa Tengah
- PKB's strongest base: 68 DPR seats nationally, majority from Jatim
- NU (Nahdlatul Ulama) has 50-90 million members nationally, concentrated in Jatim (esp. Jombang, Pasuruan, Sidoarjo, Lumajang)
- In 2024 Pilpres: East Java was a bellwether — voted for Prabowo (unusual for PDI-P stronghold areas)

### The PKB-NU Tension
**CRITICAL INSIGHT:** PKB was founded in 1998 explicitly as the political vehicle for NU, but by 2024 the relationship is deeply fractured:
- PBNU under Yahya Cholil Staquf (Gus Yahya) has explicitly said PBNU ≠ PKB
- Cak Imin (PKB) and Gus Yahya have a tense, competitive relationship
- Khofifah is PKB member but close to PBNU/Muslimat NU — not strictly a Cak Imin loyalist
- This matters: in Jatim, you can't assume all NU votes = PKB votes

### Key Political Families in Jatim
1. **Wahab Hasbullah family (Jombang)**: Mundjidah Wahab is daughter of KH Abdul Wahab Hasbullah (NU co-founder). Deep religious legitimacy.
2. **Noer family (Pasuruan/Bangil)**: Multi-generational political dynasty
3. **Azwar Anas / Banyuwangi**: Abdullah Azwar Anas (eks-Bupati Banyuwangi) now Menpan-RB in Prabowo cabinet. Wife Ipuk took over as Bupati. Classic dynasty transfer.
4. **Pramono Anung (Kediri area)**: Seskab under Jokowi, now Gubernur DKI 2024. Son Hanindhito (Dhito) is Bupati Kediri.
5. **Hasan Aminuddin family (Probolinggo)**: Both imprisoned now, but illustrates how political dynasties work in Jatim.

### Jatim Pilkada 2024 Result
- **Khofifah Indar Parawansa - Emil Elestianto Dardak** won (PKB + Demokrat + KIM Plus backing)
- **Luluk Nur Hamidah - Lukmanul Khakim** (PKB dissidents / PDI-P backed) lost
- **Significance:** Khofifah won despite conflict with Cak Imin, showing her personal brand > party machinery in Jatim
- Re-assumed office: 20 February 2025

## 🏛️ INSTITUTIONAL ANALYSIS

### KPK (Komisi Pemberantasan Korupsi)
- Founded 2002, Reformasi era institution
- Peak power: 2007-2018 under various strong commissioners
- **Significant weakening:** 2019 KPK Law revision under Jokowi — commissioners now become state employees, losing independence; oversight board (Dewas) with political appointees installed
- **Current status:** KPK under Nawawi Pomolango still prosecutes but politically sensitive cases are rarely pursued
- OTT (Operations: Tangkap Tangan/catch-in-the-act) rate has declined
- Assessment: Partially captured institution

### MA (Mahkamah Agung)
- **Major scandal 2022-2023:** Harun Masiku case (still missing, PDIP DPR candidate linked to bribery of KPU)
- Hakim Agung bribery cases — multiple judges caught accepting bribes
- Zinedine Alam (Hakim PN Semarang) - marathon sentence reduction scandal 2023

### MK (Mahkamah Konstitusi)
- **Anwar Usman controversy:** Former MK Chief Justice removed after ethical violation — changed minimum age for VP candidate (benefitting Gibran, Jokowi's son) just before 2024 election
- Anwar Usman is Jokowi's brother-in-law — direct conflict of interest
- International concern: "constitutional coup" in how Gibran's candidacy was enabled
- **Current chief:** Suhartoyo (replacing Anwar Usman)
